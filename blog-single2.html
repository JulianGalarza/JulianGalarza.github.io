"Con Las Manos" Foundation in Colombia

  • Julian Galarza
  • Volunteer

I worked every Saturday morning for 5 years going to a school in Las Cruces, one the most dangerous neighborhoods in Colombia, and meeting the 5th grade student to teach math. The Saturday started with meeting the 50 university student volunteers and checking that everything was ready. This included a check that the lunch (breakfast), donated by my university, was complete. Checking that the bus was coming on time, and finally checking that the materials for the day were ready.

After arriving at the school, the task was to organize 5 volunteers for 20 students so that there was 1 volunteer for each 4 students. Managing the logistics of one room, I was in charge of the time management, the lunch, the materials and in general that the kids have an amazing experience. Then, at the end of the day the task was to do a feedback for the students, and go back to the university. There, when the volunteers were done, I and the other staff volunteers discussed the day, created new strategies, and concluded the day.

Definitely these are some of my favorite memories, and I learned a lot. I learned what it means to help people and why it is important to try hard to reduce inequality and poverty. This fulfilled me in different ways. I made many friends who I still keep in touch with and collaborate with on other projects. This experience really sparked my passion in education, technology, and the opportunities they both provide.

Contact me if you want to know more about this project. julian.galarza@berkeley.edu
